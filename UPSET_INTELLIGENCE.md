### BARTHAG Danger Zone (Seeds 5–8)

| Zone | Sample | Upset Rate |
|------|--------|------------|
| **BARTHAG < 0.85** | n=44 | **61.4%** |
| BARTHAG ≥ 0.85 | n=228 | 41.2% |

Any 5–8 seed with BARTHAG below 0.85 is your #1 single metric to identify vulnerable favorites. Of the 44 seeds 5–8 that fell in this zone, 27 lost in the first round.

**What is BARTHAG?** Barttorvik's predicted win probability against an average Division I team. 0.90+ = elite, 0.85–0.90 = good, below 0.85 = vulnerable.

## Section 2 — Travel & Location

### Travel Distance & Direction Effect on Upset Rates

*Average underdog win rate baseline: 27.4%*

| Scenario | Upset Rate | vs Baseline |
|----------|------------|-------------|
| Favorite travels < 1,000mi | 24.5% | -2.9pts |
| **Favorite travels > 1,000mi** | **38.5%** | **+11.1pts** |
| Underdog travels East | 30.9% | +3.5pts |
| Underdog travels West | 26.2% | -1.2pts |
| Underdog > 200mi from home | 29.1% | +1.7pts |
| **Underdog < 200mi from home** | **16.2%** | **-11.2pts** |

**Key findings:**
- 🔴 **Biggest upset driver:** Favorites traveling > 1,000 miles lose 38.5% of first-round games — 14 percentage points above the baseline.
- 🟡 **Direction matters:** Underdogs traveling East win 30.9% vs 26.2% traveling West. East-coast programs perform better away.
- 💡 **Surprising finding:** Underdogs playing *near home* (< 200mi) actually win *less* often (16.2%) — smaller fan bases don't generate meaningful home-court pressure at neutral sites.

### Time Zones Crossed: Upset Rate Impact

*Crossing time zones disrupts sleep and circadian rhythm — the effect is non-linear.*

| Time Zones Crossed | Fav Loss Rate | Underdog Win Rate |
|--------------------|---------------|-------------------|
| 0 TZ (no cross) | 23.2% | 27.4% |
| 1 TZ crossed | 27.4% | 28.4% |
| **2 TZ crossed** | **54.5%** | 25.0% |
| 3 TZ crossed | 22.7% | 35.3% |

> 🚨 **The 2-Time-Zone Anomaly:** Favorites crossing exactly 2 time zones lose **54.5%** of first-round games — more than double the baseline. The most common scenario: East Coast programs (ACC, Big East) traveling to California, Colorado, or Washington. Seeds 5 and 6 account for 19 of these losses.

### AP Poll Momentum: Prior Ranking Effect on Underdogs (Seeds 9–13)

| AP Poll History | Win Rate | Sample |
|-----------------|----------|--------|
| **Was AP-ranked at some point** | **46.7%** | n=321 |
| Was never ranked | 28.3% | n=46 |

**+18.4 percentage point advantage** for previously-ranked underdogs.

Seeds 9–13 that spent any time in the AP Top 25 win the first round nearly *twice* as often as those who were never ranked. This is the single strongest predictive signal in the dataset outside of BARTHAG.

**Examples:** NC State 2024 (had been ranked), UCLA 2021 (had been ranked), VCU 2011 (reached #13 mid-season).

**How to apply it:** When evaluating a 10, 11, or 12-seed, first check if they appeared in the AP Poll at any point this season. If yes, weight them significantly as an upset pick.
