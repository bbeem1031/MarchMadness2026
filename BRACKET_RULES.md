## 🔴 MUST DO — Rules 01–05

### Rule 01 · Always pick at least one 11-seed to advance

> **64.1%** — At-large 11-seed first-round win rate

The 11 vs. 6 matchup is the single most common upset in 17 years of data — occurring **33 times** with a 51.6% win rate. Overall, 11-seeds win 50% of first-round games. At-large 11-seeds win **64.1%** of the time.

Never fill out a bracket without at least one 11-seed win. If it's an at-large 11-seed, take them every time.

**Why at-large 11-seeds win more:** They had to grind through quality competition to earn their bid. They often have higher KenPom efficiency ratings than their seed suggests — they just didn't win their conference tournament. Their resume is stronger than auto-bid 11-seeds.

---

### Rule 02 · Always pick at least one 12-seed to advance

> **43.8%** — 12-seed first-round win rate (28 occurrences)

The 12 over 5 has happened **28 times** — a near-coinflip at 43.8%. This is one of the most reliable upset patterns in the tournament. Never fill out a bracket without at least one 12-seed first-round win.

**What makes 12-seeds win:** Defense. 12-seed winners have noticeably better adjusted defensive efficiency than losers. The upset comes from locking down the 5-seed, not from offensive explosions. Look for 12-seeds with strong KADJ D ratings (see Rule 11).

---

### Rule 03 · Screen every 5–8 seed for BARTHAG below 0.85

> **61.4%** — Upset rate for seeds 5–8 with BARTHAG below 0.85 (n=44)

Any 5–8 seed with a BARTHAG below 0.85 loses 61.4% of first-round games. This is your fastest, most reliable single-metric upset filter. Check this before picking any "safe" favorite.

**What is BARTHAG?** Barttorvik's predicted win probability against an average Division I team. Think of it as overall team quality compressed into one number.
- 0.90+ = Elite
- 0.85–0.90 = Good
- Below 0.85 = **Vulnerable**

| Zone | Sample | Upset Rate |
|------|--------|------------|
| BARTHAG < 0.85 | n=44 | **61.4%** |
| BARTHAG ≥ 0.85 | n=228 | 41.2% |

---

### Rule 04 · Fade any favorite traveling over 1,000 miles

> **38.5%** — Favorite loss rate on trips over 1,000 miles (+14 points above baseline)

Favorites making a 1,000+ mile trip lose 38.5% of first-round games — 14 percentage points above the 24.5% baseline for shorter trips. Distance creates fatigue, schedule disruption, and circadian rhythm issues. Distance beats talent more often than people admit.

| Travel Scenario | Upset Rate |
|-----------------|------------|
| Favorite travels < 1,000mi | 24.5% |
| **Favorite travels > 1,000mi** | **38.5%** |
| Underdog traveling East | 30.9% |
| Underdog traveling West | 26.2% |
| Underdog within 200mi of home | 16.2% |

> 💡 **Surprising finding:** Underdogs playing *near home* (< 200 miles) actually win *less* often — only 16.2%. Playing close to home doesn't help at neutral sites the way people assume.

---

### Rule 05 · East Coast team playing in the West = upset pick

> **54.5%** — Favorite loss rate when crossing 2 time zones

Favorites crossing 2 time zones lose **54.5%** of first-round games — more than double the baseline. The most common scenario: ACC and Big East teams traveling to California, Colorado, or Washington.

Seeds 5 and 6 account for 19 of these losses. If you see a high-major East Coast team seeded 5–8 playing in the West, treat it as a near-automatic upset pick.

| Time Zones Crossed | Fav Loss Rate |
|--------------------|---------------|
| 0 TZ | 23.2% |
| 1 TZ | 27.4% |
| **2 TZ** | **54.5%** |
| 3 TZ | 22.7% |

## 🟠 STRONG — Rules 06–10

### Rule 06 · Never blindly pick a 6-seed to the Sweet 16

> **-20.3 PASE** — The worst Performance Above Seed Expectation of any seed

The 6-seed has only a **50% first-round win rate** despite being treated as a "safe" pick in millions of brackets. Their PASE (Performance Above Seed Expectation) of -20.3 is the worst of any seed in the dataset.

6-seeds are the most overrated bracket picks year after year. When evaluating a 6-seed, always look hard at the 11-seed opponent before assuming the favorite advances.

> **Exception:** A 6-seed with BARTHAG above 0.94 is a legitimate Rule 6 exception — they're seeded lower than their quality. Tennessee (0.941 in 2026) is an example.

---

### Rule 07 · Check if your underdog was AP Top 25 this season

> **+18.4 percentage points** — Win rate boost for previously AP-ranked underdogs (seeds 9–13)

Seeds 9–13 that spent **any time** in the AP Top 25 during the season win **46.7%** of first-round games vs. **28.3%** for those never ranked. That's a massive edge.

| AP Poll History | First-Round Win Rate | Sample |
|-----------------|----------------------|--------|
| Was AP-ranked at some point | **46.7%** | n=321 |
| Was never AP-ranked | 28.3% | n=46 |

A team that was ranked #18 in November and fell to a 10-seed by March is dangerous — they've beaten quality opponents and their ceiling is much higher than their bracket line suggests.

**How to apply it:** When evaluating a 10, 11, or 12-seed, first check if they appeared in the AP Poll at any point this season. If yes, weight them significantly as an upset pick.

---

### Rule 08 · Trust Big Ten and Pac-12 underdogs

> **+7.3** — Pac-12 PASE (2nd best of all conferences)  
> **+4.5** — Big Ten PASE

Both conferences are **chronically underseeded** due to the strength of their schedules inflating losses. A Big Ten or Pac-12 team seeded 10–12 likely belongs 2 seeds higher. When you see one as an underdog, take the upset.

- **Big Ten:** +4.5 PASE, most first-round upsets produced (32) in the dataset
- **Pac-12:** +7.3 PASE, consistently underseeded, great upset source

---

### Rule 09 · Fade Big 12 and Mountain West seeds

> **-15.9** — Big 12 PASE (worst of any conference)  
> **-15.6** — Mountain West PASE

Both conferences get inflated seeds due to brand reputation. Their teams routinely underperform their seeding in the tournament.

| Conference | PASE | Verdict |
|------------|------|---------|
| Big 12 | -15.9 | Worst in dataset — always look for the upset |
| MWC | -15.6 | Routinely over-seeded, strong fade |
| Big East | -7.3 | 6 champs but negative PASE — brand inflates seeds |
| A-10 | -5.8 | Worst mid-major performer |

When you see a Big 12 team seeded 5–6, look hard for the upset on the other side.

### Rule 10 · Cinderella must clear KP rank 60 and BARTHAG 0.79

> **KP < 60** — Floor for any deep underdog run (11–13 seed reaching Sweet 16+)

Every team that reached the Sweet 16 or beyond as an 11–13 seed had a KenPom efficiency rank of **60 or better** and BARTHAG of **0.79 or higher**. VCU (KP #82 in 2011) is the lone exception in 17 years.

Below those thresholds, a first or second round exit is the overwhelming outcome regardless of seed.

## 🔵 ADVISORY — Rules 11–15

### Rule 11 · For 12-seeds: defense wins more than offense

> **KADJ D** — Key differentiator between 12-seed winners and losers

12-seed upsets don't come from offensive explosions — they come from **locking down the 5-seed** below their usual efficiency. When scouting a 12-seed upset candidate, prioritize their adjusted defensive efficiency.

| Metric | 12-Seed Winners | 12-Seed Losers |
|--------|-----------------|----------------|
| KADJ EM | 13.51 | 12.57 |
| **KADJ D** | **97.4** | **98.3** |
| BARTHAG | 0.798 | 0.780 |
| WAB | 0.80 | 0.10 |

---

### Rule 12 · For 11-seeds: resume matters more than efficiency

> **NET/ELO** — Key differentiators for 11-seed winners

11-seed winners actually have *slightly lower* KADJ EM than losers — but dramatically better ELO and NET ranks. Their resume — quality wins against real competition — is what separates them.

| Metric | 11-Seed Winners | 11-Seed Losers |
|--------|-----------------|----------------|
| KADJ EM | 14.63 | 15.33 |
| BARTHAG | 0.828 | 0.836 |
| **ELO Rank** | **49.9** | **42.9** |
| **NET Rank** | **49.7** | **42.4** |

An at-large 11-seed with a strong NET rank is a genuine threat regardless of their efficiency numbers.

### Rule 13 · Expect 13–14 total upsets across the bracket

> **13.5** — Long-run average upsets per tournament (all rounds combined)

Plan your bracket around historical averages. The most chaotic years were 2021 (18 upsets) and 2014 (19). The most chalk recent year was 2025 (8 upsets through two rounds). Don't cluster all your upsets in Round 1 — spread them across rounds.

| Year | Total Upsets | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| 2014 | 19 | Most chaotic — Mercer over Duke, Dayton in E8 |
| 2021 | 18 | Oral Roberts, UCLA Final Four run |
| 2022 | 17 | Saint Peter's to Elite Eight |
| 2025 | 8 | Most chalk in recent memory |
| **Average** | **13.5** | Target for your bracket |

### Rule 15 · Respect MVC and CAA mid-majors for deep runs

> **4x** — MVC/CAA Final Four appearances as 11-seeds

The Missouri Valley Conference (Loyola Chicago, Wichita State, Drake) and Coastal Athletic Association (VCU, George Mason) have produced Final Four teams from the 11-seed line. Both consistently over-perform their seeds. If one of their programs makes the field, never auto-fade them.

## Bracket Day Cheat Sheet

### ✅ Always Do
- Pick ≥1 eleven-seed win
- Pick ≥1 twelve-seed win
- Check every 5–8 seed for BARTHAG < 0.85
- Fade favorites on 1,000+ mile trips
- East team playing in West = upset

### 🎯 Strongly Consider
- Fade 6-seeds as "safe" picks
- AP-ranked underdogs win more
- Trust Big Ten / Pac-12 dogs
- Fade Big 12 / MWC seeds
- Cinderella: KP < 60, BARTHAG > 0.79

### 💡 Keep in Mind
- Expect ~13–14 total upsets
- 12-seed wins = defense first
- 11-seed wins = NET/ELO first
- California & Ohio host more upsets
- MVC / CAA mid-majors run deep
