# THE 16 RULES FOR FILLING OUT A MARCH MADNESS BRACKET

**Data-Driven · 2008–2025 · 17 Tournaments · 1,147 Team-Seasons**  
*Rule 16 added March 18, 2026*

---

## MUST DO (Rules 01–05)

### 01 — Always pick at least one 11-seed to advance
11-seeds win 50% of first-round games. At-large 11-seeds win **64.1%**. The 11 vs 6 matchup is the most common upset in 17 years of data — 33 occurrences. Never skip it.

> **Stat:** 64.1% at-large 11-seed win rate

---

### 02 — Always pick at least one 12-seed to advance
12 over 5 has happened 28 times (43.8% win rate). It's nearly a coinflip. Never fill out a bracket without at least one 12-seed first-round win.

> **Stat:** 43.8% 12-seed first-round win rate

---

### 03 — Screen every 5-8 seed for BARTHAG below 0.85
Seeds 5-8 with BARTHAG below 0.85 lose 61.4% of first-round games. Check this before picking any "safe" favorite. It is your fastest and most reliable upset filter.

> **Stat:** 61.4% upset rate when BARTHAG < 0.85

---

### 04 — Fade any favorite traveling over 1,000 miles
Favorites making a 1,000+ mile trip lose 38.5% of first-round games — 14 points above the baseline. Distance creates fatigue, schedule disruption, and circadian issues.

> **Stat:** 38.5% fav loss rate beyond 1,000 miles

---

### 05 — East Coast team playing in the West = upset pick
Favorites crossing 2 time zones lose **54.5%** of first-round games — more than double the baseline. ACC and Big East teams playing in California, Colorado, or Washington are the highest-risk favorites in any bracket.

> **Stat:** 54.5% fav loss rate crossing 2 time zones

---

## STRONGLY RECOMMENDED (Rules 06–10)

### 06 — Never blindly pick a 6-seed to the Sweet 16
The 6-seed has the worst PASE of any seed at **-20.3**. Only a 50% first-round win rate despite being treated as "safe." The data is unambiguous: 6-seeds are the most overrated bracket picks year after year.

> **Stat:** -20.3 PASE — worst of all seeds

---

### 07 — Check if your underdog was AP Top 25 this season
Seeds 9-13 that spent time in the AP Poll win **46.7%** of first-round games vs 28.3% for those never ranked. A team that was ranked in November and fell to a 10-seed is dangerous. Biggest non-efficiency predictor in the dataset.

> **Stat:** +18.4% win rate boost for previously AP-ranked underdogs

---

### 08 — Trust Big Ten and Pac-12 underdogs
Both conferences are chronically underseeded — Big Ten **+4.5 PASE**, Pac-12 **+7.3**. A Big Ten or Pac-12 team at seed 10-12 likely belongs 2 seeds higher. Strong schedules get punished on Selection Sunday.

> **Stat:** +7.3 Pac-12 PASE (2nd best conference)

---

### 09 — Fade Big 12 and Mountain West seeds
Big 12 has **-15.9 PASE** — worst in the dataset. Mountain West is -15.6. Both conferences get inflated seeds due to brand reputation. When you see a Big 12 team seeded 5-6, look hard for the upset opponent.

> **Stat:** -15.9 Big 12 PASE — worst of any conference

---

### 10 — Cinderella must clear KP rank 60 and BARTHAG 0.79
Every team that reached the Sweet 16+ as an 11-13 seed had a KenPom rank of 60 or better **and** BARTHAG of 0.79 or higher. Below those thresholds, a first or second round exit is the overwhelming outcome. VCU (KP #82) is the only exception in 17 years.

> **Stat:** KP < 60 + BARTHAG > 0.79 — floor for any deep underdog run

---

## ADVISORY (Rules 11–15)

### 11 — For 12-seeds: defense wins more than offense
12-seed winners have noticeably better adjusted defensive efficiency than losers. The upset comes from **locking down the 5-seed**, not from offensive explosions. Look for 12-seeds with strong KADJ D ratings.

> **Key metric:** KADJ D — primary differentiator for 12-seed winners

---

### 12 — For 11-seeds: resume matters more than efficiency
11-seed winners have slightly lower KADJ EM than losers but dramatically better **ELO and NET ranks**. Their resume — quality wins against real competition — is the separator. An at-large 11-seed with a strong NET rank is a genuine threat.

> **Key metric:** NET/ELO — primary differentiator for 11-seed winners

---

### 13 — Plan for 13-14 total upsets across the bracket
The long-run average is **13.5 upsets per tournament** across all rounds. 2021 (18) and 2014 (19) were the most chaotic. 2025 was the most chalk at 8. Do not cluster all your upsets in round one — spread them across rounds.

> **Stat:** 13.5 average upsets per tournament

---

### 14 — Watch host state — California and Ohio produce upsets
California (**38.9%** underdog win rate, n=36) and Ohio (**37.5%**, n=32) are the most statistically meaningful upset states. Wisconsin (15%) and Kentucky (18.8%) are chalk-friendly. Use location as a tiebreaker on borderline matchups.

> **Stat:** 38.9% underdog win rate in California

---

### 15 — Respect MVC and CAA mid-majors for deep runs
The Missouri Valley Conference (Loyola, Wichita St, Drake) and CAA (VCU, George Mason) have produced Final Four teams from the 11-seed line. Both over-perform their seeds consistently. If one makes the field, do not auto-fade them.

> **Stat:** 4x MVC/CAA Final Fours as 11-seeds

## 🆕 HARD RULE (Rule 16) — Added March 18, 2026

### 16 — NEVER pick a team west of the Mississippi River as champion

**This is a HARD FADE rule. No exceptions.**

No team headquartered west of the Mississippi River has won the NCAA Championship in **20+ years**. The last western champion was Florida in 2006 — and specifically, no Pacific/Mountain time zone school has won since UCLA in 1995 (**31 years and counting**).

**How to apply this rule:**
- Before finalizing any Final Four or championship pick, identify which region winners are based west of the Mississippi
- **Eliminate all western schools from championship contention**, regardless of BARTHAG, seed, or analytics
- Western schools affected: Arizona, Gonzaga, BYU, Utah State, Oregon, UCLA, USC, San Diego State, Nevada, Colorado, Utah, Wyoming, New Mexico, and any other Mountain/Pacific time zone program
- **Midwest exception:** Teams in states like Iowa, Missouri, Minnesota are geographically central — apply judgment. The clearest cases are Pacific and Mountain time zone schools
- When two teams are analytically equivalent in a late-round matchup and one is a western school, **take the eastern team every time**

**Historical record:**
| Era | Last Western Champion | Years Since |
|-----|----------------------|-------------|
| Pacific/Mountain | UCLA (1995) | 31 years |
| West of Mississippi broadly | Florida (2006) | 20 years |

**2026 Application:**
- Arizona (#1 West region, 0.978 BARTHAG) — excellent team, advance to Final Four, **CANNOT be champion**
- Gonzaga (#3 West) — advance through early rounds, **CANNOT be champion**
- Any other West region team — same rule applies
- In the Michigan (Ann Arbor, MI) vs Arizona Final Four matchup, **take Michigan**

> **Rule type:** HARD FADE — override any model output that selects a western school as champion

## 🆕 ADVISORY RULE (Rule 17) — Added March 18, 2026

### 17 — Use the Trapezoid of Excellence as your championship and Final Four filter

**Created by:** Ryan Hammer (@ryanhammer09) using KenPom data

The Trapezoid of Excellence plots every tournament team on a graph of **Adjusted Net Rating (y-axis)** vs **Adjusted Tempo/Pace (x-axis)**. Nearly every national champion over the past 20 years falls within a specific zone shaped like a trapezoid, representing elite efficiency combined with **balanced, adaptable pace**.

**The core insight:** Teams at the extreme ends of the tempo spectrum — playing either extremely slow or extremely fast — are vulnerable over a 6-game championship run because they cannot adapt to different opponent styles. The trapezoid identifies teams with both the efficiency AND tempo flexibility to win six straight against varied competition.

**How to apply:**
1. Only pick teams **inside the trapezoid** to reach the Final Four
2. For the championship, focus on the top cluster: Net Rating **28+**, pace **63–72**
3. If two teams are analytically close, prefer the one more centrally located
4. Being inside the trapezoid is **necessary but not sufficient** — Rule 16 (West fade) still eliminates Arizona even though they're inside it
5. Teams just outside the trapezoid can win first-round upsets — but fade them for deep runs

**2026 Trapezoid Boundaries (Barttorvik Adj Net Rating vs Adj Tempo):**
- Net Rating floor: ~+18 points
- Pace range: ~63–72 adjusted tempo
- Shape widens at top (elite teams tolerate slightly extreme pace), narrows toward bottom

**Key 2026 Implications:**
